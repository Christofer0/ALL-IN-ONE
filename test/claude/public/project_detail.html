Project Overview

DevFlow Dashboard was born from a real pain point: our engineering teams had no single place to see what everyone was working on. We had GitHub, Jira, Slack, and a dozen other tools — but no coherent picture of team health or velocity. I set out to build that unified view.

The goal wasn't to replace existing tools, but to synthesize signals from them into a real-time, at-a-glance dashboard that engineering managers and developers alike could use without friction.

The Problem

Before DevFlow, a typical engineering manager's morning looked like this: open GitHub to check open PRs, switch to Jira to check sprint progress, skim Slack for blockers, then manually compile a status update. This ritual consumed 45–90 minutes every day.

Developers also suffered — they'd lose context switching between tools trying to understand what was blocking their PRs from being reviewed. The information existed, it was just scattered.

The Solution

I designed a three-layer architecture:

  • Data Layer: A Node.js ingestion service that hooks into GitHub webhooks and Jira webhooks, normalizing events into a unified schema stored in PostgreSQL.
  • Real-time Layer: WebSocket connections powered by Socket.io push live updates to connected clients without polling.
  • Presentation Layer: A React app with a customizable drag-and-drop widget system, letting each team configure their own dashboard view.

Redis was introduced as a pub/sub backbone to decouple webhook ingestion from WebSocket broadcasting, ensuring the system remained stable even under high event volumes during peak work hours.

Results & Impact

After rolling out to the first team of 12 engineers, the results exceeded expectations. The daily standup time dropped from 45 minutes to under 20. PR review turnaround improved by 38%. Engineering managers reported spending 60% less time compiling status reports.

Within six months, five additional teams had adopted the platform. The dashboard became a central part of our engineering culture — displayed on the office TV and referenced in daily syncs.
